1998

Laura Vinroot Poole opens Capitol in Charlotte, North Carolina with only one employee. The 800 square store was designed by her architect husband, Perry Poole.

2001

Capitol expands from an 800 square foot shop to one of 2500 square feet.

2004

Laura Vinroot Poole opens Poole Shop, a sister boutique to Capitol. Poole is geared to a young demographic, and showcases emerging designers.

2008

Capitol celebrates its 10th anniversary and moves to a 6000 square foot freestanding store.

2009

The recession hits, causing Capitol to almost go out of business. This caused a shift in the way that Capitol did business as the company fought to keep themselves afloat.

2009

Capitol hires their first Chief Financial Officer.

2009

Capitol and Poole Shop are combined, and Poole Shop moves to be located on the second floor of the Capitol building.

2012

Capitol travels with their clients to Paris for couture fashion shows and purchases.

2013

Laura Vinroot Poole and Travis Parsons launch House Account, an app that allows users to access products from specialty boutiques all around the country.

2013

Capitol becomes a boutique partner of FarFetch, an online shopping platform for luxury boutiques.

2014

Laura Vinroot Poole hosts Giambattista Valli's first United States couture show at Duke Mansion.

2014

Capitol connects with the Center for Intentional Leadership, a consulting firm dedicated to cultivating leadership development for individuals and organizations.

2015

Laura Vinroot Poole opens Tabor, a sister store to Captiol and Poole Shop that sells men's clothing, shoes and accessories.

2017

Capitol hires a Social Media and Marketing Manager to help cultivate and promote their brand.

2018

In April 2018, Capitol will be celebrating its 20th anniversary. It has grown from having only one employee to having 25.
